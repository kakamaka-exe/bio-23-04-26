Evolution Theories

A Study of Natural Science and Darwin's Legacy

01, Foundation

Explaining Prof. Dr. Dieter Vollhardt's statement

In everyday language, "theory" usually means a guess. In science, though, a theory is a well-tested explanation backed by a large body of evidence. Calling evolution "just" a theory misses this point entirely, as it is one of the most thoroughly tested ideas in all of science.

02, Context

Influences on Darwin's Theory

Darwin did not come up with his theory alone. Several scientists from his time gave him key ideas that helped shape his thinking.

Darwin's Theory of Evolution
Geology & Deep Time

James Hutton showed that landforms are shaped by slow, gradual processes over very long periods of time. Charles Lyell added that these same processes have been happening throughout Earth's entire history. This led Darwin to believe the Earth was much older than 6,000 years, giving life enough time to slowly change and evolve.

Populations & Competition

Thomas Malthus argued that populations grow faster than their food supply, so living things are always competing to survive. Darwin took this idea and applied it to all animals and plants, seeing competition as the main force behind which individuals survive and pass on their traits.

Fossils & Paleontology

Georges Cuvier showed that the history of life on Earth is recorded in layers of rock containing fossils. Finding evidence of animals and plants that no longer exist made it hard to believe life had always stayed the same, giving Darwin strong support for the idea that species change over time.

03, Comparison

Lamarck vs. Darwin

Feature Lamarck's Theory Darwin's Theory
How Change Happens Using a body part often makes it bigger and stronger, while not using it causes it to shrink over generations. Small natural differences between individuals mean some are better at surviving and more likely to pass on their traits.
The Giraffe Example Generations of giraffes kept stretching their necks to reach higher branches, so necks slowly grew longer over time. Giraffes born with longer necks could reach more food, so they survived better and had more offspring.
What Gets Passed On Changes that happen to a body during a lifetime get passed down to children. Only traits that are already in the genes can be passed on to the next generation.

Why Darwin's Theory Prevailed

Lamarck's theory was rejected because science showed that changes to a body during a lifetime do not change a person's genes, so those changes cannot be passed on to children. A blacksmith may build up strong arms over years of hard work, but his children are not born with stronger arms because of it. Darwin's explanation matched how genes and inheritance actually work, and was later backed up by the discovery of DNA.
